Methodology

How we calculate your digital health metrics

Health Scores (0-10 scale)

Overall Health

The average of all six individual metrics below. A score of 7+ indicates healthy digital consumption habits.

Content Balance

Measures how well your browsing is distributed across content types. We compare your actual consumption to an ideal distribution: 25% news, 25% educational, 20% entertainment, 15% social, and 15% professional. Sports and gaming count as entertainment; tech and business count as professional; science and reference count as educational.

Source Diversity

Counts the number of unique domains you visit weekly. Visiting 10+ different websites earns a perfect score. Low diversity may indicate echo chamber risk.

Time Management

Evaluates daily browsing time. 1-3 hours daily is considered optimal. Less suggests limited engagement; more may indicate overconsumption.

Credibility

Average credibility rating of your sources, based on our curated database of 150+ news and media outlets. Ratings consider factual accuracy, editorial standards, and transparency. Sources not in our database show as "Unknown."

Content Tone

Analyzes headlines and content for emotional tone. We detect cynical language (disaster, crisis, scandal) vs. uplifting language (progress, solution, breakthrough). Balanced consumption of both is ideal.

Political Balance

Measures diversity of political perspectives in your news consumption. We rate sources on a spectrum from far-left to far-right. A balanced diet includes perspectives from across the spectrum to avoid echo chambers.

Echo Chamber Detection

Perspective Balance

Tracks the political lean of your recent news consumption. An echo chamber alert triggers when 70%+ of your content leans in one political direction, or when you view 5+ consecutive sources with the same political lean.

Data Sources

Media Bias & Credibility Database

Our ratings for 150+ media sources are informed by independent fact-checking organizations and media bias research, including:

  • Ad Fontes Media - Media Bias Chart methodology
  • AllSides - Blind bias surveys and editorial review
  • Media Bias/Fact Check - Factual reporting and bias ratings
  • NewsGuard - Journalistic standards assessment

Note: Bias and credibility ratings represent our synthesis of multiple sources and methodologies. Individual assessments may vary.

Content Categorization

Websites are categorized based on domain analysis and our media database. Categories include: News, Social Media, Entertainment (including sports), Educational (including science and reference), and Professional (including tech and business).

Privacy Note

All analysis happens locally on your device. Your browsing data is never sent to external servers. The media sources database is bundled with the extension and does not require network access.
